The first image of NASA's Ingenuity helicopter from Mars

The US space agency NASA has released the first photo of an Ingenuity chopper fitted to a Perseverance rover sent to Mars. The helicopter, named Ingenuity, has gone to test whether rotorcraft technology can be used in the surface and atmosphere of the red planet. If successful in flight, Ingenuity would be the first rotorcraft to do so anywhere other than Earth. This is the first time that a glimpse of the Ingenuity helicopter has reached the red planet millions of kilometers away from the Earth. In the photo released, this helicopter appears to be fitted inside a cover inside the Perverence Rover. On Sunday, NASA removed the cover, after which all eyes have been seen on this helicopter. NASA has scheduled the first flight of this helicopter next month.
